Option E — Two-tier rows

recommended

Primary formats get a big-pill row of their own; the long tail moves to a second line of smaller, lighter pills. Everything stays a visible, one-click button — hierarchy comes purely from size and weight. JPG merges into the PNG panel as a format toggle.

Option F — Inline “+3 more” expander

alternative

One row with the big three plus a subtle dashed “+3 more” chip. Clicking it expands the remaining pills in place — no floating menu, no second tier. The expanded state would be remembered (localStorage), so long-tail users see their format from the second visit on.

Option G — Adaptive bar (learns your format)

bold pick

Same clean default as Option B, but the bar personalizes: export a hidden format once and it gets promoted into the visible row on your next visit (localStorage, no server state). An EPUB regular ends up with an EPUB pill; everyone else never sees it. Try it: open “More” and click “To EPUB”.

Recommendation & notes

  • Option E is the best risk/reward now that quiet-text-links are off the table: it matches the traffic tiers while keeping every format a visible one-click button — nothing hidden, no intent-page promise broken. Option G is the most interesting if you're comfortable with adaptive UI; it can be layered on top of B later without a redesign.
  • All options assume the JPG-merge: one “To PNG” button whose panel has a PNG | JPG toggle (quality slider appears for JPG). That alone removes a button from every variant and simplifies the scale-matrix rules to one surface.
  • Mobile is already tiered (Share PDF / Share DOCX + “More formats” menu) and needs no change beyond PNG/JPG entries, which are done.
  • Analytics to watch after shipping: export_hover and convert_success for EPUB/HTML/TXT — if they don’t drop, the demotion was free.
  • The “How it works” copy and hero subtitle should mention PNG once the bar is re-tiered (“export to PDF, DOCX or PNG”).
