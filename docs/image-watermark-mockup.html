Shortest. Divider does the separating; drops the verb. Cleaner, but names the tool less explicitly.

My pick: Divider + tracked caps “Made with markdown.free”, right-aligned (option 1). The hairline + all-caps + wide letter-spacing make it structurally impossible to mistake for markdown body text, links, or a heading — and “made with” states plainly that it's a tool credit. Open question for you: wording (“Made with” vs “Exported from” vs domain-only) and alignment (right vs centered), and whether you want the faint tinted bar (option 3) for the very strongest separation.
